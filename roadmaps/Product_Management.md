## MONTH 1: FOUNDATIONS (Weeks 1-4)

### Week 1: Learn Core Frameworks

**5 Essential PM Frameworks:**

1. **CIRCLES Method** (Product Design Questions)
   - Comprehend the situation
   - Identify the customer
   - Report customer needs
   - Cut through prioritization
   - List solutions
   - Evaluate tradeoffs
   - Summarize recommendation

2. **RICE Prioritization**
   - Reach: How many users affected
   - Impact: How much value created
   - Confidence: How certain are you
   - Effort: How much work required

3. **AARRR (Pirate Metrics)**
   - Acquisition
   - Activation
   - Retention
   - Revenue
   - Referral

4. **Jobs-to-be-Done (JTBD)**
   - Focus on customer goals, not features
   - Understand the "job" customers hire your product for

5. **Kano Model**
   - Basic needs (must-haves)
   - Performance needs (more is better)
   - Delighters (unexpected value)

### Week 4: Build Your First Case Study

**Case Study Format:**

**Part 1: Problem Identification**
- Choose a product you use daily
- Identify 1-2 pain points
- Research: Why does this problem exist?

**Part 2: User Research**
- Define target users
- Create user personas
- Map user journey

**Part 3: Solution Design**
- Brainstorm 3-5 solutions
- Prioritize using RICE framework
- Create wireframes (use Figma or Lovable.ai)

**Part 4: Success Metrics**
- Define North Star Metric
- List supporting metrics
- Explain how you'd measure success

**Part 5: Go-to-Market**
- Launch strategy
- Timeline
- Key stakeholders

**Case Study Ideas:**
- Improve Spotify's playlist discovery
- Add feature to LinkedIn for job seekers
- Redesign food delivery app checkout flow
- Build AI feature for productivity app

**Publish on:**
- Medium
- Personal website/Notion page
- LinkedIn post

### Week 11: Interview Prep - Behavioral Questions

**30 Behavioral Questions by Company Type:**

**FAANG (10 Questions - Leadership & Ownership)**
1. Tell me about a time you launched a product from 0 to 1
2. Describe a situation where you had to make a decision without complete information
3. Tell me about your biggest product failure
4. How do you handle conflicting stakeholder priorities?
5. Describe a time you influenced without authority
6. Tell me about a time you disagreed with your manager
7. How do you prioritize when everything is high priority?
8. Describe a situation where you had to work with difficult team members
9. Tell me about a time you used data to drive a decision
10. How do you advocate for the user when business needs conflict?

**Startup (10 Questions - Ambiguity & Scrappiness)**
1. Tell me about a time you wore multiple hats
2. How do you operate with limited resources?
3. Describe launching something with incomplete information
4. Tell me about a time you had to move fast and iterate
5. How do you validate ideas with minimal budget?
6. Describe working in a constantly changing environment
7. Tell me about a time you built something from scratch
8. How do you prioritize when everything is urgent?
9. Describe a time you had to be scrappy/resourceful
10. How do you balance speed vs. quality?

**General PM (10 Questions - Core Competencies)**
1. Why product management?
2. Tell me about yourself (2-minute pitch)
3. Describe your PM philosophy/approach
4. How do you measure product success?
5. Walk me through your prioritization process
6. Tell me about a time you shipped a successful feature
7. How do you gather user feedback?
8. Describe your ideal product team
9. How do you handle competing deadlines?
10. What's your favorite product and why?

**STAR Method Template:**
- **Situation**: Set the context (2 sentences)
- **Task**: Your responsibility (1 sentence)
- **Action**: What YOU did (3-4 sentences) 
- **Result**: Quantifiable outcome (1-2 sentences)

## SALARY NEGOTIATION GUIDE

**PM Salary Ranges (2025):**
- Associate PM: $86K-$110K
- Product Manager: $116K-$192K (avg $148K)
- Senior PM: $152K-$210K
- Principal PM: $189K-$240K

**Top Paying Companies:**
- Meta: $175K-$240K
- Google: $160K-$220K
- Amazon: $150K-$200K
- Airbnb: $170K-$230K
- Stripe: $165K-$215K

**Negotiation Scripts:**

**1. Initial Offer Response**
```
Thank you for the offer! I'm excited about the role and [Company]. 

I'd like some time to review the complete package. Could you share:
- Full compensation breakdown (base, bonus, equity)
- Benefits details
- Stock vesting schedule

I'll get back to you by [2-3 days].
```

**2. Counter Offer**
```
I'm thrilled about joining the team. Based on my research and conversations with [X people in similar roles], I was expecting something closer to [X amount].

Given my experience with [specific relevant skill] and the value I'll bring to [specific project], would [specific number] be possible?

I'm flexible and eager to find a number that works for both of us.
```

**3. Negotiating Other Benefits (if salary is fixed)**
```
I understand the base salary is set at [X]. Could we discuss:
- Sign-on bonus
- Additional equity grant
- Earlier performance review
- Professional development budget
- Remote work flexibility
- Extra vacation days
```

**Negotiation Tips:**
- Always negotiate (employers expect it)
- Never accept first offer immediately
- Get everything in writing
- Use competing offers (if you have them)
- Focus on total compensation, not just base
- Know your walk-away number
- Be enthusiastic while negotiating
