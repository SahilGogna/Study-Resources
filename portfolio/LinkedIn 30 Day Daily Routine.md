# LinkedIn 30 Day Daily Routine

## Why This Works

- LinkedIn’s algorithm rewards consistent activity, not one-off bursts
- Canadian recruiters are active on LinkedIn daily — especially at banks, consulting firms, and tech companies
- Most job seekers do only passive activity (scrolling, applying). Active users get noticed.
- 20 minutes a day is all this takes

---

## Before You Start — Profile Checklist

Do this once before starting the 30-day routine:

- [ ]  Profile photo: professional, clear background, good lighting
- [ ]  Banner image: custom or related to your field
- [ ]  Headline: not just your job title. Include what you do and who you help. Example: "Data Analyst | SQL & Python | Helping Canadian companies turn data into decisions"
- [ ]  About section: 3–5 sentences. Who you are, what you do, what you are looking for. End with a call to action.
- [ ]  Experience: bullet points, not paragraphs. Start with action verbs. Include numbers.
- [ ]  Skills: add at least 15 relevant skills. Endorse others and they will endorse you back.
- [ ]  Open to Work: turn on (visible to recruiters only is fine if you are currently employed)

## The Daily 3-Action Routine (20 minutes)

### Action 1 — Post Once (5–7 minutes)

Post something every single day. It does not need to be long.

**Easy post ideas for tech job seekers:**

- One thing you learned today (tool, concept, industry news)
- A resource you found helpful (with your take on it)
- A question for your network ("What SQL concept took you longest to understand?")
- A small win (completed a certification, finished a project)
- A repost of someone else’s content with your own 2-3 sentence comment
- A career update (started a course, attended an event, reached a milestone)

**Post format that works:**

- Line 1: Hook (bold claim or question)
- Lines 2-5: The point in short bullets or sentences
- Last line: Question or CTA ("What do you think?", "Drop a comment below")
- No hashtag spam — 2-3 relevant hashtags maximum

### Action 2 — Send 10 Connection Requests (7–10 minutes)

Do not just click Connect. Send a personalized note.

**Who to connect with:**

- Recruiters at companies you want to work at
- People in your target role at your target company
- Hiring managers at companies of interest
- Alumni from your university now working in Canada
- People you met at events (ORU sessions, meetups, webinars)

**Connection message template:**

> "Hi [Name], I came across your profile and noticed you work in [field/company]. I’m currently exploring [role] opportunities in Canada and would love to connect and learn from your experience. No pressure at all — just building my network."
> 

Keep it under 300 characters. Personalize the name and company.

### Action 3 — Comment on 5 Posts (5 minutes)

Find posts in your field using relevant hashtags (#datajobs, #canadatech, #hiring, #dataanalytics, etc.) and leave genuine comments.

**A good comment:**

- Adds a perspective ("I’ve noticed this too, especially in Canadian banking environments")
- Asks a question ("How did you handle X in this situation?")
- Shares a resource ("I’ve seen this covered well in [course/article]")

**A bad comment:**

- "Great post!"
- "Thanks for sharing"
- Generic one-liners

## Week-by-Week Expectations

| Week | What to Expect |
| --- | --- |
| Week 1 | Profile views increase slightly. A few connection acceptances. |
| Week 2 | Some post engagement starts. Recruiter views begin. |
| Week 3 | First recruiter messages or InMails if your profile is optimized. Comments from your network increase. |
| Week 4 | Noticeable increase in profile views, connection growth, and job recommendations from LinkedIn. |
