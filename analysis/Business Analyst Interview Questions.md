# Business Analyst Interview Questions (Resource Doc)

---

## Why BA in Canada

- BA is one of the most consistently hired tech-adjacent roles in Canada
- No CS degree required — many successful BAs come from business, commerce, or arts backgrounds
- Salary range: $65,000–$95,000 CAD at entry to mid level
- Top hirers: banks (RBC, TD, BMO), insurance (Manulife, Sun Life), telecom (Telus, Bell), government, and consulting firms
- Key tools: SQL, Excel, JIRA, Confluence, Visio, Tableau or Power BI

---

## Category 1 — Requirements Gathering (8 questions)

1. How do you elicit requirements from a stakeholder who doesn’t know what they want?
2. What techniques do you use for requirements gathering? (Interviews, workshops, surveys, observation)
3. How do you prioritize requirements when stakeholders have conflicting needs?
4. What is the difference between functional and non-functional requirements?
5. How do you handle scope creep during a project?
6. What is a Business Requirements Document (BRD) and what does it contain?
7. How do you validate that requirements are complete and unambiguous?
8. Describe a situation where requirements changed mid-project and how you managed it

---

## Category 2 — Process Analysis and Improvement (8 questions)

1. Walk me through how you would document a current-state business process
2. What is a gap analysis and when would you use it?
3. How do you identify inefficiencies in a process?
4. What is a SWOT analysis? Give an example of when you would use one
5. Explain the difference between As-Is and To-Be process mapping
6. What process modelling tools have you used? (Visio, Lucidchart, BPMN)
7. Describe a time you improved a business process — what was the outcome?
8. How do you measure the success of a process improvement initiative?

---

## Category 3 — Data Analysis (8 questions)

1. How comfortable are you with SQL? What types of queries have you written?
2. Walk me through how you would analyze a dataset to answer a business question
3. How do you handle missing or inconsistent data?
4. What is the difference between a KPI and a metric?
5. How would you build a dashboard to track customer satisfaction?
6. Explain a time you used data to influence a business decision
7. What BI tools have you used? (Tableau, Power BI, Looker)
8. How do you present data insights to a non-technical executive audience?

---

## Category 4 — Stakeholder Management (8 questions)

1. How do you manage stakeholders who have different priorities?
2. Describe a time you had to influence someone without direct authority
3. How do you communicate project status to senior leadership?
4. Tell me about a time a stakeholder disagreed with your recommendation — what did you do?
5. How do you build relationships with technical teams as a BA?
6. How do you handle a situation where a stakeholder keeps changing requirements?
7. Describe your approach to running an effective requirements workshop
8. How do you ensure buy-in from all stakeholders before project kick-off?

---

## Category 5 — Agile, SDLC, and Tools (8 questions)

1. What is the difference between Agile and Waterfall? When would you use each?
2. What is your role as a BA in a Scrum team?
3. How do you write a good user story? What does “Definition of Done” mean?
4. What is a sprint review vs a sprint retrospective?
5. How do you prioritize the product backlog?
6. What is UAT (User Acceptance Testing) and what is the BA’s role in it?
7. How do you create and manage a traceability matrix?
8. Describe your experience with JIRA, Confluence, or similar tools

---

## STAR Method Reminder

For all behavioural questions use:

- **S**ituation — Set the context briefly
- **T**ask — What was your responsibility?
- **A**ction — What did you specifically do?
- **R**esult — What was the measurable outcome?

Always include numbers where possible. “Reduced report generation time by 3 hours per week” beats “improved efficiency.”
