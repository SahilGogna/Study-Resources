## Who is a Product Manager?

A Product Manager figures out **"what"** to build and rallies the team to make it happen.

### Key Characteristics:

- **Leads without authority**
- **Sits at the intersection** (of business, technology, and user experience)
- **Accountable for outcomes**

> **In short:** PMs are the "CEO of the product"

---

## What does a Product Manager do?

A Product Manager's responsibilities span across multiple domains:

### Core Responsibilities:

- **Market Research** - Understanding the market landscape and opportunities
- **Problem Identification** - Discovering and defining problems worth solving
- **Ideating Solutions** - Brainstorming and conceptualizing potential solutions
- **Executing Solutions** - Turning ideas into reality
- **Stakeholder Management** - Aligning and coordinating with various teams
- **Feature Communication** - Clearly articulating product features and changes
- **Product Analytics** - Measuring and analyzing product performance
- **Product Strategy** - Setting long-term vision and roadmap

---

## Key Stakeholders

Product Managers work at the intersection of multiple teams:

- **Engineering** - Building the product
- **Design** - Creating user experiences
- **Marketing** - Promoting and positioning the product
- **Sales** - Selling to customers
- **Customer Support** - Helping users
- **Data** - Providing insights and analytics
- **Leadership** - Setting vision and strategy
- **Customer** - The ultimate stakeholder

## Product Lifecycle

The journey from idea to product follows these stages:

### 1. Market Research
**Identifying user needs and market gaps**

### 2. Problem
**Clearly defining the challenge to be solved**

### 3. Solution
**Brainstorming and designing potential resolutions**

### 4. Build
**Developing the product or feature**

### 5. Launch
**Releasing the product to the market**

### 6. Learn
**Gathering feedback and iterating for improvement**

## Exercise-1: Developing user empathy

### Question: Design a water bottle for a senior citizen?

### User Persona - Senior citizen (65+)

#### Characteristics:
- Reduced grip strength / arthritis
- Lower stamina and balance
- Diminished vision and dexterity
- Higher risk of dehydration
- May live alone or depend on caregivers

#### Pain Points with Standard Water Bottles:
- Poor visibility of water level
- Difficulty sucking or drawing water
- Difficulty opening and closing the lid
- Difficulty in holding the bottle
- High risk of spilling water

**The key is to understand the user's constraints and design a solution that addresses their specific needs.**

## How to break into Product Management

There are multiple paths to becoming a Product Manager:

### Traditional Route
1. Prepare for interviews
2. Do an MBA or a bootcamp
3. Apply for APM roles through referrals
4. Clear interviews

### Internal Transitions
- Give interviews and transition internally within your company

### New Route (Recommended!)
1. **Build a side project** or create a work product for a company
2. **Do product teardowns** of existing products
3. **Send it to hiring managers** directly
4. **Get interview calls** based on your demonstrated skills

---

## The New Route: Your 4-Step Action Plan

### 1. Product Breakdown
**Analyze apps you love**
- Study existing products deeply
- Understand what makes them successful
- Identify improvement opportunities

### 2. Create Work Products
**Write specs, user flows**
- Create PRDs (Product Requirement Documents)
- Design user flows and wireframes
- Document your thinking process

### 3. Direct Outreach
**Connect, learn, get intros**
- Reach out to hiring managers directly
- Share your work products
- Build relationships in the PM community

### 4. Build Prototypes
**Idea to interactive demo**
- Create working prototypes
- Demonstrate your ability to execute
- Show, don't just tell

## How to build?

Building products has become more accessible with AI tools:

### Start with the problem, not the solution
- Identify a real problem you or others face
- Validate that it's worth solving
- Define success metrics

### Use AI as your coding partner
- Tools like Claude, ChatGPT, and GitHub Copilot
- AI can help you build even without deep coding knowledge

### Describe in plain English
- Explain what you want to build clearly
- Break down the requirements
- Let AI help translate to code

### Iterate quickly
- Build fast, learn fast
- Get feedback early and often
- Don't aim for perfection initially

### Learn as you go
- You don't need to know everything upfront
- Learn what you need when you need it
- Build your skills through practical projects

- Find early users to test your product
- Gather feedback systematically
