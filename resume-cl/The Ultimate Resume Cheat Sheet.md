# The Ultimate Resume Cheat Sheet

A complete guide to building an ATS-friendly, impact-driven resume.

---

## Resume Format

**Length:** 1 page (1.5–2 pages only if you have **10+ years** of experience)

**File Format:** PDF

**Font:** Calibri, Arial, Helvetica, or Times New Roman (**Size:** 11–12 pt)

**Margins:** 0.5”–1”

**Color:** Stick to **black & white** (unless applying for design roles)

** Template:** Avoid tables, graphics, and columns — they confuse ATS.

> Avoid: Irrelevant details (high school, unrelated certifications, or every skill you’ve ever touched)
> 

> Recommended Template: [FAANGPath Overleaf Template](https://www.overleaf.com/latex/templates/faangpath-simple-template/npsfpdqnxmbc)
>

## Structure & Sections

### Header

✅ Include

- Full Name

- City, Province

- Email

- LinkedIn Profile

- GitHub / Portfolio (if technical)

❌ Don’t include

- Full address

- Photo

- The word “Resume”

---

### Profile Summary

> Why it matters: Recruiters spend 5–7 seconds on this section. It’s your hook — highlight skills, experience, and measurable impact.
> 

**How to write it:**

1️⃣ Start with a powerful opening statement

2️⃣ Highlight core technical skills

3️⃣ Emphasize measurable impact

4️⃣ Tailor it to the role or industry

> Pro Tips:
> 
> 
> – Keep it 50–100 words
> 
> – Use bullet points or short sentences
> 
> – Avoid generic claims — show proof of value
> 
- Example – No Experience
    - Aspiring Data Analyst with strong foundations in SQL, Python, and tableau through academic projects and certifications.
    - Passionate about leveraging data for business decisions — built a predictive model improving forecast accuracy by 15%.
    - Skilled in Excel for data cleaning, pivot tables, and dashboards, reducing reporting time by 25%.
- Example – Experienced
    - Expertise in Data Analysis & Business Insights: 3+ years improving operational efficiency by 15%.
    - Proficient in Tableau, Power BI, and Excel, creating dashboards that reduced reporting time by 30%.
    - Strong SQL & Python skills, improving forecast accuracy by 20%.

### Education

**Format:** 

Degree | University – City, Province

Month YYYY – Month YYYY

> Avoid: listing high school after post-secondary or adding unrelated course lists.
>

### Skills

**Group hard skills by type:**

- **Programming:** Python, SQL, R

- **Tools:** Power BI, Excel, Tableau

- **Databases:** PostgreSQL, MySQL

---

### Projects

> Why projects matter: They show how you apply skills in practice. Always highlight relevance and impact — not just tools.
> 

**Best Practices:**

- Showcase 2–3 strong projects

- Use **CAR (Challenge → Action → Result)** or **STAR (Situation → Task → Action → Result)**

- Always mention tools + quantify results

- Example – CAR Method
    
    **Sales Data Analysis**
    
    - **Challenge:** Seasonal customer churn was impacting revenue.
    
    - **Action:** Used Python, Pandas, and Matplotlib to analyze 1M+ transactions and identify patterns.
    
    - **Result:** Recommended strategies projected to reduce revenue loss by 20%.
    
- Example – Web Scraper Project
    
    **LinkedIn Job Posting Scraper**
    
    - Built a pipeline processing 10,000+ listings to analyze hiring trends.
    
    - Created an interactive dashboard used by 20+ classmates, helping 5+ secure roles based on insights.
    

---

### Experience

> Why it matters: Show impact, not tasks. Quantify results and align with job requirements.
> 

**Best Practices:**

- 4–5 bullet points per role (6 for most recent)

- Most recent experience first

- Use CAR / STAR framework

- If irrelevant role — focus on transferable skills and projects

- Example – STAR Method
    
    **Data Analyst Intern | ABC Inc., Toronto, ON**
    
    *May 2023 – Aug 2023*
    
    - **Situation:** Reporting was slow and error-prone.
    
    - **Task:** Improve efficiency for analytics team.
    
    - **Action:** Automated weekly Excel reports and built Tableau dashboards for real-time tracking.
    
    - **Result:** Reduced reporting time by 40% and improved data accuracy for decision-making.
    

> Formula to remember: Action Verb + What You Did + Tools Used + Quantifiable Result
>

### Certifications

Only include **proctored** ones:

- Google Data Analytics Certificate (Coursera)

- AWS Certified Cloud Practitioner

- Microsoft Excel Expert (MO-201)

### Optional Sections

**Volunteering / Extracurriculars** — show leadership or initiative.

**Example:**

*Volunteer Coordinator, TEDxXYZU*

Organized 20+ volunteers during live events, improving event flow and attendee experience.

---

## Tailoring Your Resume

- Extract **keywords** from the job posting.
- Mirror those keywords in summary, skills, projects, and experience.
- Prioritize relevant experience at the top.

## Power Verbs to Boost Impact

**General Impact:** Achieved, Delivered, Implemented, Drove, Secured

**Leadership:** Led, Directed, Managed, Supervised, Coordinated

**Communication:** Presented, Trained, Explained, Documented

**Problem Solving:** Analyzed, Diagnosed, Solved, Audited

**Creativity:** Designed, Developed, Built, Created

**Optimization:** Improved, Streamlined, Automated, Reduced

**Collaboration:** Supported, Contributed, Collaborated, Facilitated

**Data / Reporting:** Compiled, Forecasted, Tracked, Visualized

**Technical:** Engineered, Programmed, Deployed, Debugged

## Final Note

Your resume won’t get you the job — it gets you the **interview**. Keep it clear, relevant, and impact-focused. Show **value**, not tasks.
